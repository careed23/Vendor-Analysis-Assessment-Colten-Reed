EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

A systematic analysis of 386 vendors representing $7.9M in annual spend has identified three strategic consolidation opportunities totaling $850,575 in annual recurring savings (10.8% of addressable spend). Implementation is feasible within 4-6 months with standard execution risk.

KEY FINDINGS

• Total Annual Vendor Spend: $7,887,359
• Vendors Identified for Consolidation: 182 (47%) can be terminated
• High-Priority Optimization Targets: 6 major vendors representing $2.4M (31% of spend)
• Addressable Savings: $850,575 annually (conservative estimates)

THREE RECOMMENDED OPPORTUNITIES

1. CRM PLATFORM CONSOLIDATION | Savings: $623,445

Current State: Salesforce investment of $3.1M represents 39.5% of total vendor spend. Analysis suggests multiple editions, legacy instances, and underutilized licenses across the organization.

Recommended Action: Consolidate to unified Salesforce instance with negotiated enterprise licensing. Eliminate redundant editions and unused modules. Achieve 20% cost reduction through volume negotiation and license optimization.

Implementation Timeline: 12-14 weeks (4-week audit + 8-10 week execution)
Primary Risk: Data migration complexity; requires experienced implementation partner
Mitigation: Phased approach with parallel running period

2. OFFICE REAL ESTATE RATIONALIZATION | Savings: $159,000

Current State: $636K annual spend across multiple providers (WeWork, TOG, Innovent Spaces). Post-COVID environment enables hybrid work policy implementation and significant footprint reduction.

Recommended Action: Implement hybrid work policy targeting 25% space reduction. Consolidate remaining needs to 1-2 primary landlords. Renegotiate lease terms based on reduced headcount on-site. Subleash excess capacity.

Implementation Timeline: 12-16 weeks (3-4 week policy + 8-12 week execution)
Primary Risk: Employee satisfaction and retention if policy too aggressive
Mitigation: Transparent communication; offer flexibility for roles requiring office presence

3. TRAVEL & EXPENSE POLICY OPTIMIZATION | Savings: $68,130

Current State: $454K spread across travel vendors with inconsistent booking practices and limited policy enforcement. Opportunity to standardize through Navan/Tripactions platform with preferred vendor consolidation.

Recommended Action: Mandate Navan as exclusive travel booking platform. Implement pre-approval workflow. Negotiate volume discounts with preferred airlines and hotel chains. Reduce non-compliant bookings through quarterly reporting.

Implementation Timeline: 8-10 weeks (2-4 week policy development + 6-8 week rollout)
Primary Risk: Employee adoption and potential recruitment impact if policy too restrictive
Mitigation: Reasonable policy with carve-outs for legitimate business needs
